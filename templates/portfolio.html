Research Outline

Research for my portfolio site was broken down into 2 stages: the big picture and features. It is critical to understand the overall goal of any project before proceeding with development or even design. Furthermore understanding potential users and existing competitors greatly facilitates the rest of the design process and gives substance to more abstract questions that arise in design.


The first research process focused on a quick competitor (other designers) analysis of existing portfolio sites and analyzing what they did well, what could be improved, and how their site stood out. Interviews were conducted after with individuals representing the 3 major user groups I identified: industry veterans, mildly experienced individuals in the tech field, and non-technical visitors.


After identifying the core users and takeaways from the first research phase I was ready to go ahead and start planning out the tangible. Findings from the previous research artifacts guided most of the feature prioritization and allowed me to decide which features were the most desireable by users. Development difficulty was decided by experience working with similar features in the past and how I believed I would measure up. Finally, a Kano Model was added to help visualize the core features that I should be focusing on first during development.
