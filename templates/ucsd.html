University of California, San Diego App: Parking

Introducing real-time parking data to the UCSD app

The Problem

The Outline

UCSD has a powerful and versatile mobile app available for Android & iOS devices. The app development team at UCSD's technical services branch wanted to utilize recently installed 3rd party sensors which tracked the vacancy of parking spots in select parking lots on campus. The sensors can detect which specific spots are taken or not using object recognition in tandem with a system of cameras. Combined with existing knowledge of parking spot types, the system could be leveraged to recognized the type of spots available or taken. The parking & transporation department at UCSD asked the team to utilize this data in the form of an API to reduce parking congestion on campus and help students, staff, and faculty alike find parking spots.

Goals & Challenges

  • Allow users to view the parking inforation at a glance to reduce potential driving impacts.
  • Provide "fuzzy data" that is not exact, but easy to understand.
  • Show users parking data for multiple lots so they can make decisions.
  • Allow users to set their parking types, including multiple permits.
  • Allow users to choose which parking lots they want to track, up to 3.
  • Consistency with existing UCSD app styles.

  • How do we display data information without crowding the screen?
  • How do we decide between tracking parking permit types or parking spot types? Or both?
  • How do we handle empty state screens or new users who have not selected parking lots to track?
  • How do we show data for multiple permit/parking spot types at once?

The Process

The first step in approaching this project was to understand the potential users for the parking feature. From the initial brainstorming process two user groups were decided upon.

  • Students with erratic parking schedules and basic parking privileges.
  • Faculty & Staff with stable schedules and better parking permits with higher spot accessibility.
  • Visitors to the campus looking for parking were noted, but considered out of scope for the initial draft.
